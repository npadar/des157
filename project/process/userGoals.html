How will your users understand the content of the project?

I am going to try to implement a start screen where there will be images of animals roaming around their habitat and one by one they will each slowly fade out. This is meant to represent the vanishing animals on Earth due to climate change and human activity. This start screen will help the user gain an understanding of what the content of the project is. Then when the interactive map appears, the users will know that the map will represent something related to wildlife.

How will your users understand the primary objectives?

The users will understand the primary objective by reading the popups that appear when they click on a dot. A popup will appear with different tabs including information about the species, why they are endangered, and how to help. By reading the how to help page, the users will understand that they objective of this project is to inform and to encourage the users to take actions against those who are harming the environment and animals.

What content and features do your users need, and in what form do they need it?

The users need information on how they can help directly and also maybe a donate section. That way they know they can still make a difference even if they can't directly go into the field to help. There will be pictures and videos of the issues each species is facing so that the user can gain a better understanding of what is happening in the world.

How do your users think your interactive projects should work and what are their experience levels with the type of media you are creating?

My plan is to create an interactive map exhibit where the user can touch or click on a dot and a popup will appear. I think that they users will know to click around once they see a map full of dots. I don't think that they users will need a lot of experience with this because the map basically acts like a touch screen device. I believe that the majority of the target audience would have experience with touch screen devices.
